Understanding Heart Disease

Heart disease is a broad term that refers to a group of conditions affecting the heart and its blood vessels. These conditions can damage the heart, reduce its efficiency, and ultimately lead to heart failure.

  1. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): The most common type of heart disease, CAD occurs when fatty deposits (plaque) build up in the coronary arteries, which supply blood to the heart muscle. This narrowing can reduce blood flow and lead to chest pain (angina) or a heart attack.
  2. Heart Attack: A heart attack happens when a blood clot completely blocks a coronary artery, stopping blood flow to a part of the heart muscle. This can cause permanent damage and even death if not treated promptly.
  3. Angina: Angina is chest pain or discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle. It typically occurs with exertion and goes away with rest.
  4. Arrhythmias: Arrhythmias are irregular heartbeats. They can be harmless or life-threatening, depending on the type and severity.
  5. Heart Failure: Heart failure occurs when the heart weakens and can't pump blood effectively. This can lead to shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling in the legs and ankles.
